# Container Condition Comparison - New vs One-Trip vs Cargo-Worthy

Company: Atlas Container Supply
Last updated: 2026-02-28

## Quick Definitions
- Brand-new: Rare in U.S. inventory; no cargo history; premium priced.
- One-trip: Built new overseas, used once for import shipment, then sold.
- Cargo-worthy (CW): Structurally sound and wind/water tight, with cosmetic wear from service.

## What Most Buyers Choose
For most buyers, one-trip offers the best balance of appearance, lifespan, and resale value.
CW is ideal for cost-sensitive buyers who care more about function than cosmetics.

## Side-by-Side Comparison

### One-Trip
- Appearance: clean paint, minimal dents, newer floors
- Lifespan expectation: longest in field conditions
- Best for: storefront storage, schools, offices, premium projects
- Typical 40ft price point: around $3,950 plus delivery

### Cargo-Worthy (CW)
- Appearance: visible dents, scratches, rust touch-up areas
- Lifespan expectation: strong structural life when maintained
- Best for: farms, construction sites, back-lot storage
- Typical 40ft price point: around $3,250 plus delivery

### As-Is
- Appearance and structure: variable condition; inspect carefully
- Best for: low-cost non-critical use, parts, or temporary projects
- Typical 40ft price point: around $2,350 plus delivery

## Recommended Sales Narrative
If prospect asks, "What's the difference between new and one-trip?"
1. Explain true brand-new units are uncommon and expensive.
2. Position one-trip as near-new in practice and better value.
3. Offer both one-trip and CW delivered quotes for transparent comparison.
